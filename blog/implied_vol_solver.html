Model the Problem

Given market price \(C_{mkt}\) of an option, find volatility \(\sigma\) in Black-Scholes: \[ f(\sigma) = C_{BS}(S,K,T,r,\sigma) - C_{mkt} = 0 \]

Explain the Modelled Events

Volatility is the only unobservable parameter. Since \(C_{BS}\) is strictly increasing with \(\sigma\) (Vega > 0), a unique root exists.

Solution in Math (Newton-Raphson)

\[ \sigma_{n+1} = \sigma_n - \frac{f(\sigma_n)}{f'(\sigma_n)} = \sigma_n - \frac{C_{BS}(\sigma_n)-C_{mkt}}{\text{Vega}(\sigma_n)} \]

Extrapolation (C++ Implementation)

  • Initial guess: 0.5 or Brenner-Subrahmanyam approximation \(\sigma \approx \sqrt{\frac{2\pi}{T}} \frac{C}{S}\)
  • Switch to Bisection Method if Vega is small (deep OTM options), implemented in bisection.cpp
