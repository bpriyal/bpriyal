N Points on a Circle

Where geometry defines probability

Place \(N\) points independently and uniformly on the circumference of a circle. What is the probability that all points lie within a single semicircle?

Modeling the Problem

Fix one point and treat it as the start of a semicircle. For all points to lie within that semicircle, the remaining \(N-1\) points must fall within half the circle.

The Key Insight

Each point has an equal chance of being the leftmost boundary. These events are mutually exclusive.

Result

$$ P = N \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{N-1} $$
