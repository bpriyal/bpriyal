The Hopping Rabbit

Where recursion becomes structure

A rabbit climbs a staircase with \(n\) steps. It may hop either 1 or 2 steps at a time.

Modeling the Problem

To reach step \(n\), the rabbit must come from:

  • step \(n-1\)
  • step \(n-2\)

Result

$$ f(n) = f(n-1) + f(n-2) $$
